# Three-Paper Series Overview

**Author:** Juan Pablo Traverso Gianini  
**Series status:** final author manuscripts; not yet independently peer-reviewed or journal-refereed  
**Package date:** July 3, 2026

## Paper I

**Title:** *Affine Profile Reduction for Fractional Triangle Covers in Split Graphs*  
**Version:** v1.1  
**Role in the series:** methodological foundation.

Main contribution:

\[
\text{fixed-polytope concavity}
\Longrightarrow
\text{pure-profile reduction}
\Longrightarrow
\text{three-orbit optimization}.
\]

It develops continuous profile masses, heterogeneous baselines, Dirac extremization, Lipschitz stability, and the weighted three-orbit formula.

## Paper II

**Title:** *Complete-Split Extremizers for Fractional Clique Parameters on Chordal Graphs*  
**Version:** v0.8  
**Role in the series:** exact fractional chordal extremization.

It proves that the relevant chordal maxima collapse to complete split graphs, gives the exact finite triangle value, gives an exact finite theorem for general \(K_r\), and records the threshold-graph sandwich.

## Paper III

**Title:** *Linear-Error Clique Partitions of Split Graphs via Structured Triangle Packing*  
**Version:** v1.0.4  
**Role in the series:** integral linear-error theorem.

It proves

\[
\operatorname{cp}(G)\le \frac{n^2}{6}+O(n)
\]

for split graphs through bulk rounding, sparse-independent decomposition, and explicit near-extremal corridor arguments.

## Relationship

The papers are complementary.

- Paper I supplies the affine profile-space mechanism.
- Paper II gives the exact fractional chordal geometry.
- Paper III gives the strongest integral split-graph estimate in the series.

No paper should be described as replacing the mathematical role of the other two.
